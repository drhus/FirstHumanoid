Recording Notes

  • Total voiceover: ~35 seconds of speaking, ~29 seconds of silence/music
  • Target duration: 64 seconds
  • Tone: Warm, steady, slightly emotional but not melodramatic. Like telling a friend a story you can barely believe happened.
  • Pace: Slow on the hook. Build speed through beats 3-4. Slow back down for the walk.
  • Music: Emotional piano that starts soft, builds through the montage, peaks at the walk/handoff, resolves softly at the coin.
  • Captions: Every word captioned. Large, bold, white with shadow. Centered bottom third.
  • The walk (beat 6) gets 14 seconds. Don't rush it. This is what makes people share.
  • Beats 7-8 (handoff + reactions) have zero voiceover. 10 seconds of silence + real reactions = tears + comments.
  • Celebration reactions (beat 8): 2-3 quick cuts of guests reacting. Raw, candid. This drives comments ("I would be SOBBING").
  • The coin is the epilogue. "A token of memory — a small, lasting reminder that love found a way." Softer, reflective.
  • End card: "The World's First Humanoid Bridal Walk" in gold, "firsthumanoid.xyz" below it.

CapCut Effects & Transitions

  • Transitions: Use "Fade" (0.3s) between most clips. Use "None" (hard cut) for the black frame at 0:03 and the reveal at 0:25. Hard cuts = more impact.
  • Speed: Slow the aisle walk (#13) to 70-80%. Slow the reveal (#12) to 80%. Keep build montage at 100%.
  • Photos: Use CapCut's Ken Burns — tap photo → Animation → "Combo" → slow zoom in or zoom out. 2s per photo.
