Chapter I

Every Bride's Wish

There is a moment every bride imagines long before the dress, the flowers, or the rings.

The music begins. The doors open. And there, beside her, is the person who held her hand when she first learned to walk — now walking her toward the rest of her life.

For Sonia, that person had always been her grandfather. The man who lifted her onto his shoulders, who told her stories before sleep, who promised — with the certainty only a grandparent can have — that he would be there when the day came.

She never doubted it. Not once.

Chapter II

An Ocean Between Them

But life, as it sometimes does, had other plans.

Her grandfather couldn't make the crossing. Age, health, the sheer vastness of the world between them — some distances feel impossible to bridge. The dream of walking beside her seemed destined to remain just that: a beautiful, heartbreaking dream.

How do you tell someone you love that the moment they promised you — the moment you both imagined a thousand times — might not happen?

And yet, in a small corner of Malaysia, a group of people who barely knew Sonia were about to prove that love doesn't care about distance.

Chapter III

What If We Could Bridge It?

It started the way all impossible things start — with someone asking a question no one had asked before.

At Network School in Forest City, Malaysia, there lives a community of builders, dreamers, and engineers who share meals, share workshops, and share a stubborn belief that technology should serve the most human moments of all.

When they heard Sonia's story, they didn't say "that's too bad." They said something else entirely:

"What if her grandfather could still walk her down the aisle — through a humanoid robot he controls from across the world?"

The idea takes shape. A face that glows — waiting for a soul to fill it.
Chapter IV

A Community Answered

No one was asked to help. They just showed up.

Engineers stayed up late calibrating motors and testing video feeds. Someone found fabric for a suit. Someone else designed a boutonniere. They argued over camera angles and connection speeds and whether the robot's bow tie should be black or navy.

It was messy and late and beautiful — the way all acts of love are.

Chapter V

The Moment That Changed Everything

26 June 2026. The golden hour. Forest City, Malaysia.

The music began. Guests turned. And there, walking beside the bride in a tailored suit, was a humanoid robot — carrying her grandfather's face on its screen, his voice in its speakers, his tears streaming through a live connection across the ocean.

He was there. Not in body, but in every way that mattered.

Chapter VII

Their Day

Kris & Sonia.

At the center of all this — past the circuits and the code, past the late nights and the last-minute fixes — are two people who simply wanted their wedding to include everyone they love. No matter the distance. No matter what it took.

Their love inspired a community to build something the world had never seen. And it all started because one couple dared to believe that being together doesn't always mean being in the same room.

Chapter VIII

The Place That Made It Possible

Network School.

Not a corporation. Not a research lab. A community — the kind of place where someone shares a seemingly impossible idea over dinner and wakes up to find a team already working on it.

Builders, dreamers, and engineers living side by side in Forest City, Malaysia. Building the future not in boardrooms, but in kitchens and workshops and late-night conversations that start with "what if?"

This is where the world's first humanoid bridal walk was born. Because this is the kind of place where love and technology aren't opposites — they're the same thing.
